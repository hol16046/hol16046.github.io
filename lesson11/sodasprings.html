Caribou sighting in Caribou County

By Nancy Eskelson

A caribou has been spotted in caribou county, near Draney Peak. Many have discounted the sighting, as mountain caribou are endangered and have not been known to be seen south of the Salmon River area. The remainder of the sparse Selkirk herd that has resided in northern Idaho was relocated to Canada on January 14, 2019, leaving the species extirpated (extinct in a certain geographic area, but still in existence elsewhere). Skeptics have said that it couldnâ€™t have been a caribou and that the source must be mistaken. The eyewitness, Archer Phillips, remains adamant that he did indeed see a caribou, and not an elk as many have speculated. Archer was unable to obtain a photograph of the animal, but insists that the coloring and antler shape was consistent with that of a caribou. Elk have smaller antlers (only males have antlers) and dark brown necks, weigh more than caribou, and migrate long distances. Caribou have larger antlers (both male and female have antlers), weigh less than elk, do not migrate long distances, and have white/gray necks. The citizens of Caribou County will have to wait and see if further evidence of a caribou in the area can be corroborated.

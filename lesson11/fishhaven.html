Bear Lake Monster: Fact or Fiction?

By Archer Phillips

The Bear Lake Monster has been a part of local folklore since long before the first reported sighting in 1868. It has had many descriptions ranging from something like a walrus without tusks to a crocodile or reptile. Charles C. Rich contributed articles to the Deseret Evening News in the 1800s – the first published works to describe the mysterious creature. Local Native American tribes also passed down stories of the monster. Rich stated in the Aug. 5, 1868 Deseret News article that “The Indians say there is a monster animal in the Lake that has captured and carried away Indians while in the Lake swimming; but they say it has not been seen by them for many years, not since the buffalo inhabited the valley. They represent it as being of the serpent kind, but having legs about eighteen inches long on which they sometimes crawl out of the water a short distance on the shore. They also say it spirts water upwards out of its mouth.” The report continued to describe sightings of the monster(s) from local citizens. There have been additional sightings in the years since Charles C. Rich’s article, but the most recent was in 2002. Brian Hirschi, a local businessman, reported that the monster approached him while he was out on the lake. He described the monster as having green skin and red eyes. It has been nearly two decades since the last sighting – will you be the next eyewitness?

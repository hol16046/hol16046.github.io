Preston Idaho: Where the City Meets the Mountains

By Makayla Holt

Preston Idaho may seem like an insignificant town, one overlooked by outsiders and passed over by tourists. Although small, with a population of only 5,354 people, it was voted a great place to live by residents and one of the best kept secrets of the West.

Preston has an attractive setting, nearby recreation, and a diverse economy. It’s also one of the safest places to live and raise a family in the State of Idaho and Cache Valley, Utah. You may be hard pressed to find as many options for arts, culture, and entertainment as you would in a big city, but the great outdoors aren’t far away. Preston provides its own charm with the Golf and Country Club, Hardware Ranch Sleigh Rides, and the Famous Preston Night Rodeo!

Located on the northern border of Utah and the Southern border of Idaho, May, June, September, and August are the most pleasant months in Preston. High temperatures reach the high 80’s to low 90’s. December and January are the snowiest and least comfortable months of the year, with the average low around 15 degrees Fahrenheit. Preston averages 19 inches of rain and 60 inches on snow a year, but remains sunny about 205 days a year. Each season brings its own beauty and recreation opportunities.

Whether you’re looking to relocate or simply to vacation in Idaho, Preston should be on your list. It’s a great place to live if you love the great outdoors and that small-town feeling, while still being close enough to everything you need.
